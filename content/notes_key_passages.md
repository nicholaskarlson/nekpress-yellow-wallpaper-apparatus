## Scope

These notes are selective. They are not a line-by-line annotation. Each note corresponds to a passage where the story changes direction.

## Note 1.1 — “Secure ancestral halls”

P1. The opening joke about “ancestral halls” sets a tone of ironic distance. It is also a rehearsal: she tries a voice that sounds carefree before the room’s rules become visible.

## Note 2.1 — “John is a physician”

P1. The sentence is simple and devastating: the person who defines illness also defines what counts as truth.

P2. The narrator’s aside (“dead paper”) is an early statement of method: writing is the place where she can say what she cannot safely say aloud.

## Note 3.1 — “I must put this away”

P1. This is the constraint made literal. The diary is not only expression; it is contraband.

## Note 4.1 — The wallpaper’s “pattern”

P1. The pattern is described as if it were an argument that cannot be completed. The narrator returns to it because it is a problem she is allowed to have.

## Note 5.1 — “I have found out”

P1. The story turns from observation to discovery. Notice what is *not* discovered: there is no external secret. The secret is the structure of the room.

## Note 6.1 — The final movement

P1. The ending does not “explain” the wallpaper. It shows what happens when attention becomes the only remaining form of action.
