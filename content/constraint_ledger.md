## The ledger idea

This ledger treats the story as a sequence of constraints that tighten. Each constraint creates incentives: what can be said, what must be hidden, what can only be implied.

## Confinement

C1. The room is chosen “for her,” and its features are treated as neutral facts.

C2. The narrator’s discomfort is reframed as temperament.

C3. The physical space becomes the condition of speech: what can be written depends on who might see.

## Schedule

S1. “Rest” is not absence of work; it is a schedule imposed from outside.

S2. The narrator is permitted tasks that are symbolic (small, harmless) and forbidden tasks that would create agency (work, writing, decision).

S3. Schedule becomes a technology of compliance: routines that look caring but remove choice.

## Surveillance

V1. The narrator anticipates observation even when no one is present.

V2. She edits herself in advance. This is the moment where surveillance becomes internal.

V3. The story repeatedly blurs the line between “watching” and “worrying,” which is part of its cruelty.

## Self-censorship

SC1. The narrator’s voice becomes split: what she thinks, what she says, what she writes, what she hides.

SC2. The diary form becomes a survival tactic: a private record that must still dodge discovery.

SC3. This is where the wallpaper matters: a surface that can absorb attention without inviting debate.

## Breakdown

B1. When every channel of agency is closed, attention becomes the remaining “freedom.”

B2. The ending reads like an action scene, but the action is a logic completing itself.
