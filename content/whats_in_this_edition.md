This critical edition is designed for one sitting, then one re-read.

P1. The story appears first, without commentary, so the first encounter is uninterrupted.

P2. All commentary comes after the text. The goal is not to “explain the story,” but to give you tools for a second pass: what to notice, what to track, and how the narrative structure creates pressure.

P3. What you’ll find after the story:

Q1. A short critical introduction that frames the text’s central constraint: a voice that must survive inside rules.

Q2. A constraint ledger that tracks how confinement becomes schedule, schedule becomes surveillance, and surveillance becomes self-censorship.

Q3. Scene cards for a guided re-read, plus selective notes on key passages.

Q4. Seven short context essays (2–4 pages each) that connect the story to domestic medicine, property, labor, narration, and publication history.

Q5. A glossary and reader’s guide for book clubs or classroom use.

Q6. A small “metrics snapshot” generated from pinned analysis code in the public MIT apparatus repo (linked from NEKpress.ca).
