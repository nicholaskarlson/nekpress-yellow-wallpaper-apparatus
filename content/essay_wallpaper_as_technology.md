## Wallpaper as a technology of repetition

Wallpaper is built for repetition. Its purpose is to turn a surface into a stable pattern, a background. In the story, that purpose fails. The narrator refuses the wallpaper as background and turns it into foreground.

P1. Repetition becomes pressure. The pattern does not resolve; it loops. The narrator’s attention loops with it.

P2. The wallpaper becomes interactive. Not because it moves, but because it can be reread endlessly. It becomes the one “problem” the narrator is allowed to work on.

## Attention as the last remaining agency

If you cannot choose your work, you can still choose what you notice. Attention becomes a form of autonomy. The story shows both the power and the danger of this: attention can be protest, but it can also become self-consuming.

## Pattern, interpretation, and authority

Interpretation is the narrator’s expertise. Authority tries to deny her expertise by denying the legitimacy of her perceptions. The narrator therefore becomes her own investigator: observing, hypothesizing, revising, insisting.

R1. In a second read, track how often she shifts from “it seems” to “I know.”

R2. Notice that the shift is not a revelation of external facts; it is a claim to interpretive authority.
