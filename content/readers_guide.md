## Book club plan (one meeting)

P1. First 10 minutes: each reader names one sentence that felt “normal” on first read and “alarming” on second read.

P2. Next 20 minutes: use the constraint ledger. Pick one link in the chain (confinement → schedule → surveillance → self-censorship → breakdown) and find two passages that support it.

P3. Next 20 minutes: use the scene companion. Choose one card and argue both sides: (A) the narrator is describing a social reality; (B) the narrator is describing an inner reality. Then ask how the text blends them.

P4. Last 10 minutes: discuss the ending as logic. What small dismissals does it accumulate?

## Classroom module (50 minutes)

R1. Warm-up: identify the “rules of the room” in pairs.

R2. Close reading: pick a paragraph where the narrator hedges (“perhaps,” “of course,” “I suppose”). Discuss hedging as strategy.

R3. Writing prompt: write a one-page constraint ledger for a modern situation (school, work, online life). Keep the chain idea.

## Discussion prompts

Q1. Where does care become control?

Q2. What counts as evidence in the story?

Q3. What does the narrator gain by writing, and what does she risk?

Q4. What does the wallpaper allow her to do that she cannot do elsewhere?
