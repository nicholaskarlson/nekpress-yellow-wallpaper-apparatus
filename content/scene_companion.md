## How to use these cards

Read the story again with one card per scene. The goal is not to “solve” the wallpaper. The goal is to track what the narrator can and cannot say.

## Card 1 — The bargain

P1. Re-read the opening paragraphs and underline every instance of “of course,” “naturally,” or “one expects.”

P2. Question: Who is the implied audience? Who is she persuading?

## Card 2 — The room as device

P1. List the room’s features as if they were neutral.

P2. Then rewrite the list as if it were a design brief for control.

## Card 3 — The writing problem

P1. Mark every time she mentions writing, hiding, stopping, or being interrupted.

P2. Question: What does “rest” require her to give up?

## Card 4 — The medical voice

P1. Identify moments where diagnosis is asserted without evidence.

P2. Question: How does certainty function as authority?

## Card 5 — The social performance

P1. Note when the narrator performs gratitude, calm, or humor.

P2. Question: What emotions are allowed, and which are punished?

## Card 6 — Pattern and attention

P1. When the wallpaper becomes “interesting,” what has just happened in the social world?

P2. Question: Is the wallpaper escape, protest, or both?

## Card 7 — The last pages

P1. Track verbs. The ending accelerates. What does the acceleration *replace*?

P2. Question: What kind of “freedom” is being claimed, and at what cost?
