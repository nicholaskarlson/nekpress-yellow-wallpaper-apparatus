## A story with an argument

The story was written into a world where “nervous illness” was widely discussed, and where domestic ideals shaped what counted as a good life for women. The text is not merely descriptive; it is critical. It stages a conflict between lived experience and institutional certainty.

## Reception and misreading

Stories like this are often misread as simple tales of “madness.” That reading turns the narrator into a medical object and repeats the very logic the story criticizes.

P1. The narrative asks you to interpret the *social* situation, not only the psyche.

P2. The ending is not a twist; it is an exposure. The exposure is of authority’s limits: what authority cannot perceive, it labels irrational.

## Why publication context matters

Publication is part of the text’s meaning. When a story about discounted testimony circulates, it invites readers to become witnesses. The apparatus in this edition is meant to support that witnessing: to give vocabulary for the constraints the story dramatizes.
