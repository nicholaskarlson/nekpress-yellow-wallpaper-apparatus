Confinement — Restricted movement and reduced choice inside a physical boundary.

Constraint — A rule or condition that narrows available actions and shapes incentives.

Credibility — The social status granted to testimony; who is believed and why.

Domestic medicine — Household governance framed as health management.

Idleness — Enforced absence of meaningful work; not the same as rest.

Interpretation — The act of making meaning from patterns; a form of expertise.

Legibility — Being seen and evaluated through someone else’s categories.

Rest cure — A deprivation-based protocol framed as care and protection.

Schedule — Time structured by authority; routines that remove choice.

Self-censorship — Editing oneself in anticipation of consequences.

Surveillance — Observation and the anticipation of observation; external or internal.

Witness — A reader who treats testimony as evidence rather than symptom.
